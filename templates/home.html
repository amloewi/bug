bug
An insistent text message reminder service
bug reminds you of something, repeatedly, until you do it. Like a person would.

Because that's how things get done.

Examples

Buy groceries for tomorrow!
If you sent bug this text, it would be sent back to you every 30 minutes, 5 times. Those are the defaults.

Gym closes at 6 today 60 3
You'd get this message 60 minutes apart, 3 times, because you gave bug instructions for both of those things.

Dinner with Paul at 8. 45
Messages are 45 minutes apart, but there are still the default 5 of them. The period keeps bug from thinking '8' is an instruction, but you can use anything that's not a number.

When you set a reminder, bug assigns it a name, like 'beetle', and sends you a message to tell you what that name is. You can use that name to stop the reminder, if you finish the thing you wanted to do (congratulations!) or cancel it, if you made a mistake.


Did beetle/ Cancel ant
This would stop jobs called 'beetle' and 'ant,' respectively. 'Did' keeps a record of the reminder, while 'Cancel' doesn't.
